Early Life


Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He came from a humble background and had a deep interest in science from a young age.

Career


Kalam worked as a scientist at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was responsible for the development of India’s first satellite launch vehicle. He also played a major role in India’s nuclear program.

Achievements


  • Developed India's first satellite launch vehicle
  • Led India’s nuclear weapons tests
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007
  • Author of multiple books, including "Wings of Fire"

Legacy


Dr. Kalam is remembered for his contributions to science and technology and his inspiring leadership as the President of India. His vision for a developed India continues to motivate millions.
